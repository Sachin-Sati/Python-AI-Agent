The butterfly effect refers to the idea that small, seemingly insignificant events can have a significant impact on a larger system or outcome. For example, the flapping of a butterfly's wings can cause a hurricane on the other side of the world. This concept is often used to describe the unpredictability of complex systems and the potential for small changes to have large and far-reaching consequences.
